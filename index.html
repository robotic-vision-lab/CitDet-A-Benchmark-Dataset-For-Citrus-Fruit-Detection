CitDet: A Benchmark Dataset for Detecting Citrus

Jordan A. James1          Heather K. Manching2          Matthew R. Mattia3          Kimberly D. Bowman3          Amanda M. Hulse-Kemp2,4          William J. Beksi1
1The University of Texas at Arlington         2North Carolina State University         3Subtropical Insects and Horticulture Research Unit, USDA Agricultural Research Service         4Genomics and Bioinformatics Research Unit, USDA Agricultural Research Service

Abstract

We present a new dataset to advance the state of the art in detecting citrus trees affected by the Huanglongbing (HLB) disease in orchard environments. Significant progress has been made in solving the fruit detection problem in recent years, however the lack of publicly available datasets has complicated direct comparison of results. While citrus detection has been of interest in current agricultural research, there is an absence of work involving citrus affected by HLB. As such, much of the research on citrus detection is only applicable to orchards located in specific areas of the world not affected by the disease. To address this issue, we enhance state-of-the-art fruit detection methods for use in typical orchard settings by providing high-resolution images of citrus trees affected by HLB, along with high-quality bounding box annotations of citrus fruit. Fruit on both the trees and the ground are labeled to allow for identification, which contributes to advancements in yield estimation. The dataset contains over 30,000 bounding box annotations for fruit instances contained in 600 high-resolution images.
